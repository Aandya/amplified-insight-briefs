Amplified Insight Bajaj Finance — Credit Cycle Diagnostic
25 June 2026 · Vadodara
Diagnostic Verdict · Pyramid Principle · Recommendation First
Bajaj Finance's Q4 FY26 GNPA improvement from 1.21% to 1.01% is a provisioning artefact, not an organic credit cycle reversal. Three structural risks are simultaneously building beneath the headline: an undisclosed 12-month-on-book vintage cohort now entering the default peak window, a suppressed rural early-delinquency signal, and a geographic funding mismatch that creates a bilateral squeeze — credit stress rising from below, potential HNI depositor flight from above — with no natural hedge between them.
Investor recommendation: do not add to position until three named metrics are disclosed (12MOB vintage, rural 0–30 DPD, NCD spread vs. Oct 2024 baseline). Reduce if NCD spreads widen further — the bond market is the honest signal in this system.

The provisioning artefact mechanism: GNPA improves in two ways — either borrowers genuinely pay down their dues (organic recovery), or loans are written off and removed from the GNPA numerator (provisioning action). If Q4's improvement was write-off-driven rather than collection-driven, the reported 1.01% is clean on paper while the economic loss has already been absorbed. The Stage 2 → Stage 3 migration in Q3 (watchlist converting to NPA, not recovering) is inconsistent with an organic collection story. The CFO cited "vintage improvements across 3MOB, 6MOB, 9MOB" — but refused 12MOB, which is where the FY24–25 peak-growth cohorts are now sitting.

The NCD signal (most actionable): Bajaj Finance's NCD spreads widened during the risk-weight period (pre-April 2025). The CFO confirmed they have "normalised somewhat" — not returned to baseline. The Taleb read (Antifragile — from the consulting vault): Bajaj Finance's loan book is concave — bounded upside (interest income), unbounded downside in a credit cycle turn. The NBFC that grows in Extremistan using Mediocristan models underestimates tail risk by construction. The reported GNPA of 1.01% is a Mediocristan number — averaged, smoothed, provisioned. The bilateral squeeze scenario (rural stress + HNI depositor flight simultaneously) is Extremistan — non-linear, co-dependent, self-amplifying. The Damodaran read (Corporate Life Cycle — from the vault): Bajaj Finance is in late-growth phase transitioning to mature. The Damodaran CEO mismatch thesis applies: leadership running growth-phase playbooks (AUM expansion, customer acquisition, new segment entry) during a credit cycle turn that requires harvest-phase thinking (vintage quality, provisioning depth, funding structure). The three-decision framework (invest / finance / divide) — at this stage — should weight financing discipline and capital preservation. The current management posture weights investment (gold loans, new branches, deposit franchise growth) while the financing structure (17% deposits, 83% market) remains unchanged. That sequencing is the LP4 gap.
